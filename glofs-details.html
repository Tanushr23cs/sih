Detailed Information about GLOFs

Glacial Lake Outburst Floods (GLOFs) occur when a dam holding back a glacial lake fails suddenly, releasing a massive amount of water and debris downstream. This can result in significant flooding and destruction. GLOFs are a serious natural hazard in areas with glacial lakes, often leading to catastrophic consequences for communities and ecosystems downstream.

These floods can be triggered by various factors including glacier melting, heavy rainfall, or seismic activity. The rapid release of water can lead to downstream flooding, property damage, and loss of life. Early warning systems and emergency preparedness are crucial in mitigating the impact of GLOFs.

Precautions to Take During a Flood:

  • Move to higher ground immediately and stay there.
  • Avoid walking or driving through floodwaters.
  • Keep away from electrical equipment and power lines.
  • Follow local news and emergency services instructions.
  • Prepare an emergency kit with essential supplies.
